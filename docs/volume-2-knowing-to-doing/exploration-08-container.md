# Eighth Exploration: The Container — Preparing the Conditions for Hearing in the Community


![](images/image-007.jpeg)

## What a Container Is
The container is the prepared interior space that a group of men or women creates together before doing any significant spiritual work. We call it a container because it is like a vessel — it holds what happens inside it, creates a boundary between the sacred work and the ordinary noise of life, and provides the structural conditions for genuine encounter. It is not a magic circle or a ritual formula. It is a set of relational commitments that, when genuinely entered into, change the quality of what can happen inside them.

Let me tell you how Dave Smith describes opening one of these containers, because the theory is only half of it. The other half is the room.

We would start standing in a circle — everyone on their feet, which matters more than it sounds. Standing brings a different kind of energy than slouching on a couch. Someone holds a walking stick in the center, and every man puts one hand on it. That physical point of contact does something I still can’t fully explain — it creates a shared focus before anyone has said a word. We open with short prayers, inviting the Holy Spirit in, and then we move to the first real question: “What are you feeling right now?” Not “how are you doing,” which gets you “fine.” Feeling. And the practice is to name the emotion — glad, angry, sad, afraid, ashamed — and maybe say a sentence about it. That’s it. No stories yet, no explanations. Just where you actually are in this moment.

From there, each man has the option to “put out” something that might block his presence — the argument he had in the car, the deadline sitting on his chest — by naming it and physically gesturing it out. And then to “bring in” a blessing or an intention. These are short, strong, and real.

Finally, before any work begins, each man makes eye contact with every other man in the circle — five to ten seconds each — and asks himself silently: Can I share my heart with this man tonight? Am I safe here? Can I give to him and receive from him? If the answer is yes for everyone, you say, “I’m clear.” If it isn’t, you say so, and you deal with it before going further. What Dave calls man-to-man clearing — working through an actual conflict or residue between two men, in front of the group, using a facilitator — is one of the most powerful things I’ve seen in a group setting. The container cannot hold genuine work if there is unaddressed static between its members.

None of this is ritual for its own sake. Each step maps directly onto the four conditions above. The emotional check-in moves you from your head into the present — it is the embodied practice of the Presence condition. Putting out and bringing in clears the channel. The eye contact surfaces anything in the way of the Safety and Clarity conditions before the work starts. When all four conditions are genuinely in place, what happens next is often surprising, and almost always good.

The four conditions of a genuine container are Safe (includes confidential), Present, Clear, and Intentional. I want to connect each of these to the spiritual laws they implement.

## Safe: The Hearing Condition
***Rom. 14:3-4 (ESV)***

*"Let not the one who eats despise the one who **abstains, and** let not the one who abstains pass judgment on the one who eats, for God has welcomed him. Who are you to pass judgment on the servant of another? It is before his own master that he stands or falls."*

Safety in a container is not the absence of challenge; it is the assurance that I will not be judged as a person while I am doing my work. I can say what is actually true about my interior life without the fear that what I reveal will be used against me or published outside the container. This is the condition that makes genuine sharing — and therefore genuine hearing — possible. A person who is managing the risk of exposure cannot simultaneously be open to the Spirit. The two orientations are mutually exclusive in the moment.

## Present: The Attention Condition
***Rev. 1:8 (ESV)***

*"'I am the Alpha and the Omega,' says the Lord God, 'who is and who was and who is to come, the Almighty.'"*

Presence is the act of bringing my full attention into this specific moment, with these specific people, in the awareness that the Great I AM is present here. The practice of naming my current emotional state at the start of a container is a simple but effective technique for moving from conceptual presence ("I know I should be here") to actual presence ("here is what is actually happening inside me right now"). The naming anchors me in the present rather than in my thoughts about the past or future.

## Clear: The Channel Condition
***Matt. 7:5 (ESV)***

*"You hypocrite, first take the log out of your own eye, and then you will see clearly to take the speck out of your brother's eye."*

Being clear means I have no conscious barrier between myself and God, and no unaddressed issue between myself and the other men. The log metaphor is relevant: if I am carrying an unacknowledged judgment of another person in the group, my hearing in that group is degraded. I am perceiving through the log rather than seeing clearly. The clearing process is simple: before the work begins, I identify anything that is between my God and me, or between another person and me, and I address it. I do not need to process it fully; I need to acknowledge it and set it aside so it does not occupy my attention during work.

## Intentional: The Obedience Condition
***John 5:19 (ESV)***

*“**So** Jesus said to them, ‘Truly, truly, I say to you, the Son can do nothing of his own accord, but only what he sees the Father doing. For whatever the **Father** does, that the **Son** does likewise.’”*

This is Jesus’ own description of the intentional posture. He did not come into a room with an agenda; He came with eyes open to what the Father was already doing. Intentionality in a container is that same advanced declaration: I am here, I am watching, I am willing to do whatever you show me, regardless of whether I had it in my plan. A man who enters a container with conditions on his obedience — “I will do whatever God asks as long as it is not this specific thing” — has not actually entered the container. He is still outside it, negotiating.

**Proposed Law (Structural): The container — the set of relational conditions (Safe, Present, Clear, Intentional) established before community spiritual work — is the practical implementation of the Vol 1 hearing channel law in a group context. Each condition removes a specific class of interference: Safety removes the fear of exposure; Presence removes the distraction of past and future; Clarity removes the degrading effect of unacknowledged judgment; Intentionality opens the obedience channel in advance. When all four conditions are genuinely in place, the group’s collective hearing capacity is significantly greater than the sum of individual capacities.**

**Certainty: 80% ***The four conditions are well-established from practice. The claim that collective hearing exceeds the sum of individual hearing is consistent with the NT model of communal discernment (Acts 13, 1 Cor 14) and with my direct observation in groups that have genuinely achieved all four conditions.*

**FORMATION DOCUMENT CONNECTION: The four container conditions (Safe, Present, Clear, Intentional) represent the small-group implementation of what MSFIG describes as the conditions for corporate hearing and obedience at Levels 3–5. MSFIG’s small group taxonomy explains that Level 3 is when trust enables the group to shift from discussing God’s Word to discerning God’s voice together — and each of the four container conditions directly tackles a specific obstacle to that transition. Safety removes the fear of exposure that keeps people at Level 2 (performing for approval). Presence removes distractions from ****the ****past and future that prevent genuine attention to the Spirit’s movement. Clarity eliminates the degrading effect of unacknowledged judgment that turns discernment into performance. Intentionality acts as the group’s equivalent of the Vol 1 obedience channel — opening the hearing channel in advance by choosing to obey before knowing the details of the prompting.**
