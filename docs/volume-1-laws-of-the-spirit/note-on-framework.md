# A Note on the Framework: The AQAL Question

I have been implicitly using Ken Wilber’s AQAL framework as an organizing scaffold — interior/exterior on one axis, individual/collective on another, with my addition of a z-axis for the Spirit dimension (positive toward God, negative toward the adversary). I want to flag a concern I have about this approach before I build more on it.

Wilber’s framework is humanistic at its base. Its metaphysical ground is Buddhist — specifically, its apex is "formless awareness" equated with various eastern concepts of ultimate reality. More significantly, the entire AQAL architecture is organized around the developing self as its own center. The self moves through levels of development, integrating more perspectives, expanding its capacity. The self is the protagonist.

Scripture’s anthropology is the opposite. The self is not the center. The self is created for a relationship with and surrender to God. The correct orientation of the self is not self-development but self-donation. These are not compatible metaphysical foundations.

This does not mean the AQAL grid is useless to me. The four-quadrant structure (interior/exterior, individual/collective) is a genuinely useful organizing map for categorizing where different spiritual dynamics operate. I plan to keep the grid. But I need to be careful to use it as a tool and not inherit its metaphysical assumptions. The z-axis (Spirit dimension) is the critical correction — it subordinates all four quadrants to a reality that transcends and constitutes them. That is no longer the AQAL framework. It is a Christian adaptation of one of its structural features, focused on the Spirit at work in us.

I hold this at about 80% certainty: the four-quadrant map is a useful organizing tool; the AQAL metaphysical framework is not compatible with a Christian investigation of the Laws of the Spirit and should not be adopted. More than that, there may be more than just a Z-axis extension. Why might a two or more-dimensional extension be more appropriate? We serve an infinite God. He very possibly has an infinite number of dimensions and connections in his creation.
