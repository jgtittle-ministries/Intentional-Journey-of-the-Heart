# A Word to My Kids at the End of This Volume

I started this journey individually in the 80s and 90s and as a small group in 2002 with three guys in my living room on Saturday mornings and a systems dynamics modeling tool. Over twenty years later, the journey has taken me to places I did not expect — into electrical engineering analogies for spiritual force, into Ken Wilber’s AQAL framework and its limits, into the mathematics of feedback loops and resonance, into the physics of supersets and projections.

And every trail has brought me back to the same place: a deeper, more specific, more practically grounded sense of awe at the God who built all of this. The early scientists were right — discovering the laws of the natural world was, for them, a way of knowing the Father better. That is what this investigation has been for me. Every law I find does not reduce the mystery; it deepens it and makes it more personal.

I do not know what you will do with all of this, if anything. But I believe the life Jesus offered us — where miracles are normal, where we walk in daily conversational intimacy with the Father, where we see what He is doing and join Him in it — is not a special dispensation for a handful of mystics. It is the intended operating mode for any person willing to get into alignment with how His world actually works.

I’m still working on the alignment. I expect I will be for the rest of my life. That is part of what makes getting up in the morning so interesting.

*Blessings — Daddy and Granddaddy*
