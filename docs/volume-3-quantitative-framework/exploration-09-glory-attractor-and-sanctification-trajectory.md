# Ninth Exploration: The Glory Attractor and the Sanctification Trajectory

## Glory as the Fixed Point of the Entire System
TFT’s most important contribution to the Vol 3 project may be the formal identification of Glory as the eschatological attractor, the stable fixed point toward which the entire field configuration evolves. In dynamical systems terms, an attractor is a state toward which nearby trajectories converge over time, regardless of starting point, as long as the trajectory remains within the basin of attraction.

The scriptural grounding for Glory as attractor is robust:

***2 Cor. 3:18 (ESV)***

*“And we all, with unveiled face, beholding the glory of the Lord, are being transformed into the same image from one degree of glory to another. For this comes from the Lord who is the Spirit.”*

The transformation is directional (one degree of glory to another), asymptotic (approaching but not reaching full Glory within the temporal frame), and mechanism-specified (the Spirit is the dynamic mediator who moves us along the trajectory). This is exactly the language of a dynamical system approaching an attractor: directional, gradient-following, with the Spirit as the vector field that defines the gradient.

The practical implication for Vol 3: every spiritual practice, every heart-clearing process, every act of obedience, every encounter with genuine community, all of these are interventions in the sanctification trajectory. They are not arbitrary good works; they are applications of force along the gradient that points toward the Glory attractor. Understanding the shape of the gradient is what allows intelligent spiritual formation rather than random effort.

## The Sanctification Trajectory in Configuration Space
TFT describes the sanctification trajectory as a path through the transcendental manifold, beginning at a “Fallen Configuration” (low values on the Truth, Goodness, and Beauty axes in a distorted region) and winding upward through stages toward the Glory attractor. The path is not straight because the manifold is curved; the coupling parameters mean that growth in one dimension produces curvature in the path, pulling it toward or away from the attractor, depending on whether the coupling is being honored.

This maps directly onto my calling from the Vol 2 training plan: daily hearing practice grows the Truth axis; community accountability grows the Goodness axis; worship grows the Beauty axis. The reason all three are needed is that the attractor is in three-dimensional space; movement along only one or two axes does not reliably approach a target in the third.

## The Three Taxonomies as a Unified Formation Map
My doctoral research claims that sharpens the Vol 3 project considerably: the three taxonomies that Bloom and his collaborators developed — cognitive (Bloom, 1956, revised 2001), affective (Krathwohl, Bloom, and Masia, 1964), and psychomotor (Simpson, 1972) — correspond to three distinct but interdependent dimensions of human formation that must all be engaged for genuine spiritual development. Neglecting anyone produces a characteristic deformation.

Mapped onto the TFT framework and the IJH operational vocabulary:

- The cognitive taxonomy corresponds to the Truth axis of TFT’s configuration space. One discipline that develops it is preaching, the accurate presentation of the Word that builds the mental model of who God is and what He has done. The goal is the renewal of the mind (Rom 12:2). Neglecting this axis produces a person with a genuine desire for God but without the truth framework to understand what He is saying or to test what they hear.
- The affective taxonomy corresponds to the Goodness and Beauty axes together, the internalization of values (Goodness) and the reorientation of desire (Beauty). The discipline that primarily develops it is worship, and the framework that describes its stages is the Krathwohl taxonomy: from bare awareness (Level 1) through active response (Level 2) through intrinsic valuing (Level 3, the critical threshold) through integrated worldview (Level 4) through full characterization (Level 5). The goal is ordered desires and a will aligned with what is genuinely good. Neglecting this axis produces a person who knows the truth but does not desire it, the Pharisee problem Jesus identified in Matthew 23.
- The psychomotor taxonomy corresponds to the action dimension: the repeated bodily practice of hearing and obeying until obedience becomes automatic. The disciplines that develop it are the full set of formation practices in Vol 2 — PROAPT, the Garden of Your Heart, the clearing tools, and the community accountability structure. The goal is what James K.A. Smith (2009) calls the sanctified imagination: habits of perception and action so thoroughly formed that they function before deliberation, responding to the Spirit spontaneously rather than only when consciously prompted.
Paul Bramer’s observation in my research is directly relevant to the Vol 3 measurement program: “Christian education has had a tendency to focus on cognitive knowledge acquisition, while Christian formation much more deliberately brings in the affective domain.” The IJH project has always understood this instinctively — the Saturday morning workshop was never a lecture series; it was a formation community. But the three-taxonomy framework gives us the formal vocabulary to say precisely why: cognitive alone is insufficient because it develops only one axis of the three-dimensional attractor space. The formation program must be all three.

## The Consummation: Attractor Reached
The attractor is only fully reached at the eschaton. Within the temporal frame, the trajectory asymptotically approaches but does not reach the Glory fixed point; there is always another “degree of glory” available. This is not a defect of the system; it is its nature. The joy of the journey is the journey itself: each step closer to the attractor changes what I can see, what I can do, what I can love, what I can know.

***Rom. 8:18 (ESV)***

*“For I consider that the sufferings of this present time are not worth comparing with the glory that is to be revealed to us.”*

The eschatological glory is the attractor fully reached: Truth, Goodness, and Beauty all at their maximum values simultaneously, with Love (the meta-coupling) binding them in perfect coherence, and all of creation participating in the result. This is the new creation of Revelation 21-22, described in TFT’s language as the global minimum of the cosmic potential, the state where all forces are at rest in perfect equilibrium because all things are perfectly ordered.

**Proposed Law (Eschatological): The entire spiritual dynamic of creation, individual sanctification, communal formation, the work of the church, and the operations of spiritual force are structured by the Glory attractor. Every genuine movement toward Truth, Goodness, and Beauty (in their coupled form, not isolated) is a movement toward the attractor. The attractor is real, approachable, and the destination that gives meaning to the entire journey. Every miracle along the way is a local preview of the final configuration: the natural world momentarily aligned with the spiritual world, and everything in its right place.**

**Certainty: 85%   ***Among the highest-confidence statements in Vol 3. The scriptural grounding is explicit and robust. The TFT formalization of Glory as an attractor is a genuine clarification of what the scriptures already describe. The mechanism of approach (the sanctification trajectory) is **medium-confidence**. The consummation is **high-confidence** theologically, though its specific dynamics are beyond what any model can describe.*
