# Taxonomy Key: How This Volume Classifies Its Explorations

Volume 3 inherits the full taxonomy from Vols 1 and 2 and adds a new quantitative layer. Each Exploration in this volume is classified according to the type of law it proposes, using the following extended taxonomy. I include the inherited types for completeness so you can see how the whole system fits together.

## Inherited from Vol 1: Structural, Operational, Gateway Laws
The full foundation for this taxonomy is laid in Volume 1’s Taxonomy Key; readers new to this volume should start there before proceeding.

These three types carry forward from Vol 1. Structural Laws describe the landscape (in Vol 3, this is now formalized as TFT’s transcendental manifold — Truth, Goodness, and Beauty as coordinate axes). Operational Laws describe movement through that landscape. Gateway Laws describe the conditions that must be present for any movement to be properly oriented. When a Vol 3 Exploration is tagged with one of these types, it is proposing a formal, potentially quantitative version of a law type already established in Vol 1.

## Inherited from Vol 2: Diagnostic and Developmental Laws
Diagnostic Laws (describing blockage conditions) and Developmental Laws (describing growth trajectories) appear in Vol 3 when the quantitative framework illuminates them. The Affective Taxonomy, introduced in Vol 2 as a descriptive framework for developmental stages, becomes a measurement protocol in Vol 3 — the primary instrument for operationalizing spiritual distance.

## New in Vol 3: Field/Quantitative Laws
A Field/Quantitative Law proposes that a spiritual relationship can be expressed not just qualitatively but as a measurable quantity or relational equation. This is the core ambition of Vol 3. The certainty ratings for Field/Quantitative Laws are lower than for other types — this is appropriate and honest, not a failure. The program is correct even where the specific equations remain provisional. When you see a Field/Quantitative tag, hold the specific mathematical form loosely while taking the underlying relational structure more seriously.

## New in Vol 3: Eschatological Laws
An Eschatological Law describes the final state toward which all the spiritual dynamics are oriented — what TFT calls the Glory attractor. These laws are not primarily about present operational strategy; they describe the destination that gives meaning to the entire journey. There is one primary Eschatological Law in this volume (Exp. 9), and it carries the highest confidence rating in the volume. The eschatological frame is not a speculative addition; it is the load-bearing context that makes all the other laws intelligible.

## How This Taxonomy Completes the Three-Volume Arc
The three-volume taxonomy now forms a complete arc. Vol 1 mapped the territory (Structural Laws) and described how forces move through it (Operational Laws). Vol 2 described what blocks those forces (Diagnostic Laws), what clears the blockages (Tool-Application Laws), and how capacity develops over time (Developmental Laws). Vol 3 asks: Can all of this be expressed precisely enough to make testable predictions? The Field/Quantitative type is the answer to that question — partial, provisional, and honest about its own limits, but the right program for the next generation of exploration.

**VOL 1 CONNECTION****:  ***The** entire Vol 3 quantitative project rests on Vol 1’s foundational laws as its empirical data set. Every equation proposed in Vol 3 is an attempt to formalize a relationship first identified qualitatively in Vol 1. The Trust Stock model (Vol 1, Exp. 1), the nested person structure (Vol 1, Exp. 2), the Authority force-multiplier (Vol 1, Exp. 7), and the Prayer Resonance model (Vol 1, Exp. 8) are the four primary inputs to Vol 3’s spiritual force equation.*

**VOL 2 CONNECTION****:  ***Vol** 2’s Affective Taxonomy formal map (Vol 2, Exp. 7) and Community as Amplifier law (Vol 2, Exp. 9) are the two most important Vol 2 contributions to the Vol 3 quantitative framework. The Affective Taxonomy becomes the measurement protocol for spiritual distance. The **Community Amplification law becomes the third primary feedback loop in the systems dynamics model. Vol 2’s emotional knot model (Vol 2, Exp. 2) is formalized in Vol 3 as local **minima** in the potential energy landscape of TFT’s transcendental manifold.*

**The Formation Documents and the Quantitative Program**

Volume 3 asks whether the laws developed qualitatively in Volumes 1 and 2 can be expressed quantitatively. The Formation Documents are relevant here in a specific and important way: they provide the best current candidates for the measurement protocol. SST proposes that the Affective Taxonomy’s five stages are the most tractable method for operationalizing spiritual distance — because they describe observable behavioral indicators, not just interior states. HFT proposes a concrete pilot instrument: self-report Likert scales combined with external witness reports from small group members and a spouse, close friend, or spiritual director. MSFIG notes that the affective domain has been the slowest adopted in educational practice precisely because measurement is hard — but Hubbard’s assurance applies here too: clarity about what is being asked makes measurement possible, and the Formation Documents provide that clarity in more detail than any prior source in the IJH project.
