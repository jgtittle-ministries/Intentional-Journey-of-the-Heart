# Sixth Exploration: Conservation Laws — The Hardest Question in Vol 3

## Why Conservation Laws Matter
In mature physical theory, conservation laws are among the most powerful and useful results. They tell you what cannot change, which constrains enormously what can change, which makes prediction possible. Conservation of energy, momentum, charge, and baryon number — each of these puts a hard constraint on physical processes and eliminates whole classes of impossible outcomes without calculating the details.

Emmy Noether’s theorem establishes that every conservation law corresponds to a symmetry of the underlying Lagrangian: if the system’s behavior is unchanged under some transformation (translation in time, rotation in space, phase shift), then some quantity is conserved. If TFT’s Lagrangian has symmetries, it has conservation laws. The question is: which symmetries does it have, and what are the corresponding conserved quantities?

I hold the following section at lower certainty than anything else in this volume. I am doing what the Vol 3 project is supposed to do at the frontier: identifying the shape of the question, proposing candidate answers, and being honest about which are hypotheses and which have scriptural grounding.

## Candidate Conservation Law 1: The Parable of the Talents — Spiritual Capacity is Not Conserved When Unused
***Matt. 25:29 (ESV)***

*“For to everyone who has, more will be given, and he will have an abundance. But from the one who has not, even what he has will be taken away.”*

I think this is the most remarkable non-conservation statement in the entire corpus of scripture. Physical energy is conserved when unused; a battery sitting on a shelf does not lose charge by not being used (ignoring self-discharge). But spiritual capacity appears to be anti-conserved when unused: the servant who buries his talent does not merely fail to gain; he loses what he had. Spiritual capacity is not preserved by hoarding; it is preserved only by use.

The implication for the Vol 3 equations: spiritual energy is not a conservative force in the physical sense. It does not satisfy a simple conservation equation. Instead, it behaves more like a biological capacity, a muscle that atrophies without exercise, a language that degrades without practice. Use preserves; disuse destroys. This is a positive feedback loop: using spiritual capacity builds capacity; not using it diminishes capacity. To put it bluntly, to sit on it is to lose it.

**TFT CONNECTION: ***In TFT terms, this would appear as an asymmetric flow in the field dynamics; the potential landscape is not symmetric under time reversal. Moving toward the Glory attractor increases field stability; moving away or stagnating decreases it. This is consistent with TFT’s **treatment of Glory as a genuine attractor (a stable fixed point toward which trajectories converge), which implies that the basin of attraction **is itself** dynamic; the further from the attractor you are, the harder it is to stay where you are.*

## Candidate Conservation Law 2: The Widow’s Oil and the Loaves — Abundance Exceeds Conservation
***2 Kgs. 4:5-6 (ESV)***

*“**So** she went from him and shut the door behind herself and her sons. And as she **poured** they brought the vessels to her. When the vessels were full, she said to her son, “Bring me another vessel.” And he said to her, “There is not another.” Then the oil stopped.”*

The oil stopped when the vessels ran out, not when the original quantity was exhausted, but when the capacity to receive ran out. This is a natural law violation from within the natural frame (a finite quantity of oil fills indefinitely many vessels), but from within the spiritual frame it follows a different conservation law: the constraint was not on the quantity of oil available (which was effectively unlimited from within the spiritual frame) but on the receptive capacity of the system (there were only so many vessels).

The loaves and fishes (Mark 6:41-44) follows the same pattern: the constraint is on the number of hungry people, not on the food itself. The food multiplies until all are satisfied, and then there is excess. The natural conservation law (energy is conserved: you cannot create food from nothing) is superseded by a higher-order principle. This is the Miracle Frame from Vol 1’s Opening Exploration: miracles are not violations of natural law from within a higher frame, but from within the natural frame alone; they appear as violations of conservation.

The Vol 3 implication: in the spiritual frame, abundance is the default condition, and scarcity is not a fundamental constraint. The constraint on miraculous provision is not the availability of God’s resources but the receptive capacity of the system, my faith, the vessels I have prepared, and the community that can receive and distribute. This is the widow’s principle: bring as many vessels as you can, because the oil will fill whatever you bring.

## Candidate Conservation Law 3: The Confession-Restoration Pair — Blocked Capacity Is Restored
From Vol 1’s Open Trail, now partially developed in Vol 2: unconfessed sin loads the channel, reducing the effective flow of spiritual power. Confession restores the channel. The question for Vol 3 is whether this is a conservation statement: is something conserved through the confession-restoration cycle, or is something genuinely created or destroyed?

My current hypothesis: the underlying capacity (what TFT would call the position in configuration space) is conserved through confession, but the blockage has prevented it from being expressed. Confession does not create capacity; it removes the obstacle that was preventing existing capacity from flowing. This is analogous to clearing a clogged pipe: you did not create new water pressure; you removed the obstruction that was preventing the existing pressure from pushing water through.

If this is right, then spiritual capacity in the full sense (position in the transcendental manifold) is something like conserved across the confession-restoration cycle, with the blocked state representing a kind of potential that cannot become kinetic until the blockage is cleared.

**Proposed Conservation Framework (Low Certainty): In the spiritual world, (1) capacity that is used tends to grow; capacity unused tends to diminish — an anti-conservative dynamic, unlike physical energy. (2) God’s provision in the spiritual frame is not constrained by natural conservation laws; it is constrained by the ****receptive capacity of the created system. (3) The confession-restoration cycle conserves the underlying spiritual position but removes the obstacle that was preventing that position from expressing as an effective force and power. These three are not a single conservation law but a family of related observations that will eventually ****resolve**** into a more unified framework.**
