# Seventh Exploration: Systems Dynamics as the Modeling Language

## Why This Tool and Not Another
I have been using Causal Loop Diagrams since the 1980s, particularly with the Saturday morning workshop group. The choice was not arbitrary: Systems Dynamics is specifically designed to model complex systems where multiple variables interact through feedback loops with time delays, which is exactly the structure the IJH laws suggest. The Rom 10:17 chain is a stock-and-flow. The Obedience Channel is a reinforcing feedback loop (obedience opens the channel, which increases hearing, which increases faith, which enables more obedience). The Emotional Knot is a balancing feedback loop that has become a trap. Systems Dynamics is the right language for all of these.

The question Vol 3 must ask is whether we can move from qualitative Causal Loop Diagrams to quantitative stock-and-flow models that make actual predictions. A qualitative CLD tells you the sign of each relationship (does A increase B, or does A decrease B?) and identifies whether a loop is reinforcing (positive feedback) or balancing (negative feedback). A quantitative model specifies the functional form and the magnitudes, so that it can be simulated and its predictions checked against data.

## What Would a Quantitative Spiritual Dynamics Model Require?
Four things would be needed to move from qualitative to quantitative:

- Operationalized measures of the stock variables. The primary stocks in the model are: faith/trust (measured by something like the REVEAL continuum, supplemented by the behavioral indicators from the Vol 3 draft notes — financial giving patterns, prayer frequency, willingness to share faith, response to persecution); spiritual distance (the three-axis measure proposed in Exploration 4); and emotional knot load (potentially measurable through the Vol 2 tool framework — number of identified and unprocessed knots, depth of each).
- Measured flow rates. How fast does genuine hearing fill the faith stock? How fast does persistent prayer increase resonance? How fast does an emotional knot load drain available bandwidth? These require longitudinal data from communities willing to be measured.
- Identified time delays. Time delays are the source of the most counterintuitive dynamics in complex systems: they cause oscillations, overshoot, and the decoupling of cause and effect, making the system hard to understand intuitively. The Vol 3 draft notes correctly identify time delay as one of the most important features of the spiritual system. Daniel’s 21-day delay (Dan. 10:13), Elijah’s seven-prayer sequence, the entire structure of “waiting on the Lord” — these are all time delay phenomena.
- Validated model structure. The causal diagram must be tested: do the feedback loops actually produce the behavior patterns we observe in spiritual communities? If the model predicts that a community with high word-input but low obedience should show slowly increasing faith followed by plateau and decline, does that match what the REVEAL data shows in churches with high Scripture-teaching but low application emphasis? This is the testing methodology.

## The Primary Feedback Loops
### The Faith-Obedience Reinforcing Loop
Word → Hearing → Faith (trust stock increases) → Action (obedience) → Revelation (next word arrives) → Word → … This is the primary positive feedback loop in the system. It is self-amplifying when running: each cycle of hearing and obeying opens the channel wider for the next. But it is also fragile at the entry point: if the word does not actually arrive, or if the hearing is blocked (Vol 2’s blockage mechanisms), or if the action step is skipped, the loop stalls.

### The Knot-Bandwidth Balancing Trap
Emotional knot present → Bandwidth consumed (hearing capacity reduced) → Less genuine hearing → Less faith development → Less capacity to receive healing for the knot → Knot persists → Bandwidth consumed → … This is a balancing loop that has become a trap — a stable configuration that resists change from within the system. The only way to break it is an external input (the ministry of the Holy Spirit through the Vol 2 tools) that injects truth directly into the knot, bypassing the degraded bandwidth channel.

### The Community Amplification Loop
Individual spiritual force → Shared in community → Community tests and confirms → Collective faith increased → Collective action produces larger results → Results build community’s faith → More willing to share and test → Individual more willing to bring → … This is a reinforcing loop with a time delay (results take time to materialize) and a safety valve (the correction mechanism from Vol 2’s community law prevents runaway amplification of error).

**Proposed Modeling Law: The qualitative Causal Loop Diagram of the ****IJH**** system contains *****at minimum***** three primary loops: a reinforcing Faith-Obedience loop (the engine of spiritual growth), a balancing Knot-Bandwidth trap (the primary obstacle), and a reinforcing Community Amplification loop (the multiplier). Moving from qualitative to quantitative requires operationalized measures of the stock variables, measured flow rates, identified time delays, and model validation against longitudinal community data. This is a multi-year research program, not a single study.**

## The Spiral Trajectory: Why Progress Looks Like Regression
One of the most important modeling corrections the doctoral research demands is this: the system does not move through configuration space in a straight line. The Affective Taxonomy research is explicit that real progress is spiral, not linear. A person at Level 4 who suffers a major loss may appear to regress to Level 1 or 2 — questioning everything, disengaging from practices, feeling nothing in prayer. In a naive linear model, this looks like the feedback loop running backward: the faith stock depleting, the channel closing, and spiritual distance increasing.

But this misreads what is actually happening. Walter Brueggemann’s analysis of the Psalms identifies the correct pattern: orientation → disorientation → reorientation. The disorientation phase is not a regression; it is a necessary stage in the formation of a deeper orientation. The roots are being driven further down, not pulled up. Saint John of the Cross describes the same dynamic in the Dark Night of the Soul: the Spirit strips the person of their dependence on spiritual consolations precisely so that the faith can be rebuilt on a foundation that does not depend on feelings or visible results.

In systems dynamics terms, the correct model is not a monotonic stock-and-flow but a system with resonant perturbation: the trust stock temporarily depletes as an old, shallower form of trust is dissolved, and then refills at a higher level as a deeper form takes hold. This is similar to the quantum tunneling phenomenon — the system temporarily occupies a lower-energy state during the tunneling event, then emerges into the true lower-energy state on the other side of the barrier. The disorientation is the tunneling event.

The practical modeling implication is significant: a longitudinal measure of spiritual distance that shows a temporary decrease during a period of suffering does not necessarily indicate the system is moving away from the Glory attractor. It may be moving through a transition that will resolve at a shorter spiritual distance than the pre-crisis state. The model must include a “suffering state” variable that distinguishes between trajectory-interrupting suffering (sin, unresolved grief, worldly distraction) and trajectory-deepening suffering (the Dark Night, the disorientation phase). The two require different interventions and different predictions.
