# Fifth Exploration: Spiritual Energy and Power

## The Classical Definitions and Their Spiritual Parallels
In physics, work is force applied through a distance (W = F × d). Energy is the capacity to do work. Power is the rate at which work is done (P = W/t). These three quantities are related but distinct. The distinction matters for the spiritual calculus because conflating force, energy, and power produces confusion about what prayer does, what faith is, and what the Gifts of the Spirit are for.

Proposed spiritual parallels:

- Spiritual force (F_s): What causes movement (velocity, acceleration) in spiritual distance — movement along the Truth-Goodness-Beauty axes toward the Glory attractor. As developed in Exploration 3.
- Spiritual energy (E_s): The accumulated capacity to apply spiritual force over time. Trust as a stock (the Vol 1 stock-and-flow language) is the primary accumulator of spiritual energy. When I act in faith, I am releasing stored spiritual energy — which is why disciples sometimes “run out” and need to be refilled, and why Jesus went away to pray before and after major ministry events.
- Spiritual power (P_s): The rate at which spiritual energy is effectively released — what James 5:16 calls prayer “as it is working.” High spiritual power means a high rate of effective faith-action; low power means slow or intermittent engagement.
***Acts 1:8 (ESV)***

*“But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the end of the earth.”*

The word for power here is dunamis — the same root as dynamite, the capacity for explosive effect. This is not spiritual force (F_s) in my taxonomy; it is the Spirit-given amplification of spiritual power (P_s) — the rate at which the disciples could effectively release spiritual energy in witness and in the charismatic gifts. Pentecost dramatically increased P_s; the Vol 2 channel-clearing work increases the efficiency with which P_s can flow.

**TFT CONNECTION: ***TFT classifies Power (**δύν**αμις) as the potential energy or driving term **V(**φ, χ, η) in the Lagrangian — the stored energy that becomes kinetic when released. This maps directly onto my taxonomy: spiritual energy as stored potential, spiritual power as the kinetic release of that potential. TFT also notes that Power without Holiness becomes demonic, which is the constraint equation **H(**φ, χ, η) ≥ 0 limiting the accessible regions of configuration space. Vol 1’s Holiness as gateway law is TFT’s boundary condition in action.*

**EXEGETICAL GROUNDING NOTE — The NT Vocabulary of Energy:  **The Greek word family built on the root energe- — energeia (active working, operation), energeo (to work, to be at work in), energēma (the result of working) — appears repeatedly in Paul’s letters in contexts that bear directly on what this Exploration is trying to name. God is the one who energei in believers both to will and to act (Phil. 2:13). Paul labors, striving according to his [God’s] energeian that works powerfully in him (Col. 1:29). The surpassing greatness of God’s power toward believers is described through three compounding terms — energeia, kratos, ischus — in Eph. 1:19–20, and the same energeia that raised Christ from the dead is the operative force. In Eph. 3:20 it is God’s dynamis working (energoumenēn) within us that accomplishes “more than all we ask or imagine.”

Christian Schwarz, in the first volume of the Energy Trilogy (God’s Energy, Vol. 1: Reclaiming a New Testament Reality, 2021), argues that Western Christianity has systematically translated this word family away — rendering energeia as “working,” “activity,” or “power” in ways that strip out its operational, dynamic connotation — and that this suppression has contributed to the flattening of exactly the spiritual dynamics this Exploration is trying to recover. The attempt in these pages to speak with precision about spiritual force, energy, and power is therefore not theologically novel. It is recovering a New Testament vocabulary that has been largely dormant in the Western church.

This connection deserves more extended treatment than is possible here and is flagged as a high-priority integration task.

## The Limited Bandwidth Problem
One of the most practically important observations in the Vol 3 draft is this: I have finite emotional and spiritual bandwidth at any given time. This is not a failure; it is a design feature. An electrical circuit has a finite current-carrying capacity. Exceeding that capacity produces heat, not more useful work.

The practical implication: if I spend my spiritual bandwidth on complaining, grieving unresolved losses, or maintaining emotional knots, I have less available bandwidth for the operations of faith, hope, and love. This is not guilt-inducing; it is a systems observation. The Vol 2 work of clearing emotional knots is not just morally helpful; it is spiritually strategic. Clearing a knot returns bandwidth to the pool available for spiritual operation.

***Heb. 12:1 (ESV)***

*“...let us also lay aside every weight, and sin which clings so closely, and let us run with endurance the race that is set before us.”*

This scripture is not exhortation alone; it is a systems design principle. The weight (even when not sin) costs me bandwidth. Laying it aside increases my available capacity for the race. The runner who is carrying extra mass is not morally inferior to the runner who has laid it down, but they are energetically disadvantaged. The Vol 2 heart work is how we lay down the weight.

**HYPOTHESIS: Spiritual energy is a finite resource within any given time frame, replenished primarily through the Word, worship, and prayer (the Vol 1 inflows into the faith stock), and depleted by active spiritual operation and by the maintenance cost of unresolved emotional knots. The practice of regular clearing (Vol 2’s monthly practice) is therefore not optional but necessary for sustained high spiritual power.**

**Certainty: 70% ***The finite bandwidth observation is consistently supported by scripture and by the experience of extended spiritual ministry. The specific depletion mechanism and replenishment rates are open trails. The cost of maintaining emotional knots is my inference from Vol 2’s model, medium confidence.*
