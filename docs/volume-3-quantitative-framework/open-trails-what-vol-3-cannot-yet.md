# Open Trails: What Vol 3 Cannot Yet Answer

The following are the most promising open trails identified in this volume. I am listing them in order of what I believe is their importance and their tractability — how likely they are to yield significant new understanding with sustained effort.

## High Priority — Most Tractable
- The Affective Taxonomy Measurement Protocol: The most tractable near-term measurement project is to run the PROAPT-based small group assessment (Vol 2, Exploration 7) with systematic Affective Taxonomy level tracking, and correlate the results against REVEAL stage data from the same congregation. The hypothesis: the Affective Taxonomy Level 3 threshold (intrinsic valuing of scripture) maps to the MOVE “Growing to Close” transition. If this correlation holds across multiple communities, it provides the first quantitative bridge between the affective formation literature, the MOVE empirical data, and the IJH operational laws. This is a doctoral-level research project that is ready to run.
- **The Minimum Factor Protocol: **Christian Schwarz’s NCD framework applies Liebig’s Law of the Minimum — originally a biochemical principle stating that plant growth is determined not by total nutrients available but by the single nutrient in shortest supply — to church health diagnosis. The NCD Church Survey identifies a congregation’s single weakest quality characteristic and directs intervention there first, on the principle that raising the minimum factor produces disproportionate overall gains. Schwarz calls the resulting growth movement “all-by-itself growth” — once the rate-limiting constraint is removed, the system grows by its own internal momentum.
- The IJH analog is promising enough to be named as a specific research trail. The emotional knots and cognitive distortions of Vol 2 are plausible individual minimum-factor candidates: a person whose formation is blocked by an unresolved wound, a distorted image of God, or a persistent pattern of avoidance may show little response to any formation practice until that specific blockage is addressed — not because they lack faith or discipline, but because the barrel cannot fill past its shortest stave. The research task is to determine: (1) whether a person’s or small group’s minimum factor can be identified early in the formation process, the way the NCD Survey identifies it for congregations; and (2) whether targeted intervention in that specific area produces the disproportionate formation gains NCD documents at the church level. If so, the minimum-factor diagnostic logic becomes one of the most practically useful tools in the Vol 4 testing protocol.
- The Mustard Seed Resolution: Determine definitively whether the threshold model (Model A) or the proportionality-above-threshold model (synthesis) better fits the scriptural data. This requires systematic examination of every healing and miracle narrative in the Gospels and Acts, looking specifically at what Jesus says about the faith involved.
- The Quantitative CLD: Build the first quantitative stock-and-flow model of the primary IJH feedback loops using Vensim or equivalent tool. Calibrate against REVEAL longitudinal data. Test specific predictions. The model must include the spiral perturbation dynamics for suffering events, not just linear stock-and-flow.
## High Priority — Harder
- The Conservation Laws: Identify the symmetries of the spiritual Lagrangian (in TFT’s framework) and determine what Noether’s theorem implies about conserved quantities in the spiritual domain. This requires collaboration with someone trained in mathematical physics and familiar with both quantum field theory and the TFT framework.
- The Time Delay Analysis: Systematic examination of time delay phenomena in scripture (Daniel’s 21 days, Elijah’s seven prayers, the Lazarus four days, the widow’s oil sequence) to characterize the typical dynamics of spiritual time delays. What determines the length of a delay? What sustains faith through it? How do delays interact with resonance?
## Important — Longer Horizon
- The Community Resonance Model: Extend the individual force equation to collective force. What determines collective spiritual force in a community? Is it simply additive (sum of individual forces), or does genuine community coherence produce superlinear amplification? Matt. 18:19 (“if two of you agree on earth about anything they ask”) suggests the latter.
- The Gifts of the Spirit as Force-Amplifying Mechanisms: Systematic analysis of each gift (1 Cor 12) as a specific mechanism for amplifying spiritual force in a specific domain. Prophecy as Truth-axis force; healing as Power applied through the Holiness boundary; tongues as resonance-establishing in a domain below conscious will. This requires both exegetical and experiential work.
- The Field Equations: What are the spatial propagation equations for spiritual force? How does spiritual force propagate through a community, through a family, through a culture? Does it follow inverse-square-law attenuation (like gravity and electromagnetism) or something different? The Daniel 10 account of spiritual warfare suggests that propagation is not instantaneous and can be impeded, which implies both a propagation equation and a spiritual friction or impedance equation.
## A Note to Whoever Picks This Up Next
I started the IJH project in early 2002 in my living room in a community of three other men. Amanda wrote about it in January 2003. In 2026, I am still on the same journey, twenty-four years later, and the frontier is still far ahead. I expect this work will not be finished in my lifetime. I am documenting it as faithfully as I can so that whoever comes next — whether it is one of you kids or grandkids, or someone I have not met yet — can pick it up at the frontier and keep going.

One dimension of the project I have not yet developed, but that research in Christian leadership ethics will eventually need to address, is the ethical implications of operating in spiritual force.

Let me at least name what needs to be developed, even if I can’t complete it here. The ability to influence others spiritually — through prayer, through prophecy, through the authority structures of the spiritual world — carries ethical obligations that flow directly from the Vol 1 Authority law. If I am operating in delegated authority from the Son, then I am operating as a steward, not an owner. The force is not mine. The authority is not mine. The results are not mine. This means three things at minimum: I cannot use spiritual authority for my own ends, only for the mission for which it was delegated; I am accountable to the authority structure above me (Jesus, and the community He has placed around me) for how I exercise it; and I bear special responsibility for the protection of those who are more vulnerable — in spiritual capacity, in emotional openness, in power differential — than I am.

The gnostic community warning in Volume 2 is the closest I have come to this territory. A community that closes its feedback loop begins using spiritual practices for its own internal coherence rather than for the King’s purposes. That is a specific form of spiritual authority misused. Future work on this will need to engage the literature on spiritual direction ethics, on pastoral abuse, and on the theology of servant leadership — not as a separate ethical category bolted onto the IJH project, but as the necessary practical application of what Vol 1 Exploration 7 already implies.

The vision is worth the pursuit: a life in which miracles are the norm, not the exception. A life in which we understand enough about how the Spirit works that we can cooperate intelligently with what He is doing rather than stumbling around hoping to get lucky. A life that looks, at least in the direction of, what Jesus modeled in the gospels.

That vision did not start with me, and it will not end with me. That is exactly how it should be.
