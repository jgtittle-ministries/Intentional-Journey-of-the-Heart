# The Four Foundational Principles

Before I get into any specific explorations, I want to lay out the four principles I treat as axioms for this investigation. I borrowed the axiom language from mathematics — just as Euclidean geometry builds outward from a small set of starting assumptions, I am working from four starting assumptions that I hold with roughly 90% certainty. Further exploration may refine or deepen these, just as Einstein’s work refined Newton without discarding him. That is fine with me. These are working axioms, not dogma.

I want to register a caution about my own title before you go further. C.S. Lewis used legal language brilliantly in Mere Christianity to point toward God — but he was careful to note that once you arrive at God via the law, you discover you have not arrived at a lawful system. You have arrived at a Person who wants you as a child, not as a subject. The laws I am describing in these volumes are, in my opinion, real and discoverable and operable — but they are the structural features of a love relationship, not a spiritual technology to be mastered. A person could learn all eight laws in Volume 1, execute the training plan offered in Volume 2 with discipline and precision, and still have missed the entire point if they have pursued the laws rather than the Lawgiver. I keep this caveat close because I know my own tendency — the engineer’s tendency — to love the system more than the source. The laws are meant to get you closer to Him, not to substitute for Him.

**1. Laws of the Spirit exist. **The spiritual world operates according to regular, predictable, lawful relationships — as orderly as the physical world, because the same God created both.

2.	**These Laws are knowable. **We will not see all of them, but we should be able to see more than we do today. The wind blows where it wishes — but we should be able to see more than just the sound of it passing.

3.	**These Laws are knowable in all three “worlds” simultaneously **— in my spirit, in my mind and emotions, and in my body and actions. If I have truly grasped a law of the Spirit, my doing will change. Otherwise, I have only memorized it, which is different. Said another way, if I am not changed by God’s word, then I haven’t yet heard God’s word.

4.	**We can operate in these Laws.**** **This is not merely an intellectual exercise. The goal is to live closer to the life Jesus modeled — where miracles are not the exception but the expected fruit of operating in alignment with how God’s world actually works.
