# A Note on Two Kinds of Laws

As I have developed these explorations, I’ve realized I have been working on two fundamentally different kinds of laws, and I need to be clear with myself and with you about that distinction, because conflating them creates confusion.

The first kind I will call **structural laws**. These describe some of what exists in the spiritual world and how its components are organized, what the elements are, and how they relate to one another architecturally. The relationship of Spirit, Heart, Soul, and Body is a structural law. The grouping of Faith, Hope, and Love as an eternal cluster is structural. These are more like topology — describing the shape of the territory.

The second kind I will call **operational laws**. These describe causation and process, how things move and change over time, and what causes what. Romans 10:17 is an operational law: Word → Hearing → Faith. If-then statements are typically operational. These are more like dynamics that describe how forces and flows work in the territory.

Both kinds are important, and they interact. But the methodology for investigating them differs, and so do the questions you ask. I will try to label each exploration clearly going forward.
