# Future Development

Speaking of future explorations and relative certainty, all of Vol 3 (Spiritual Dynamics) is open for challenge. Transcendental Field Theory (TFT) needs someone with the right background in Quantum Field Theory math and who is also scripturally grounded to review and test the concepts and implications. Vol 2 is a collection of heart work processes that I and others have tested over many years, and that can be very effective. This was actually where my journey started, at the small-group process level. It was later that the connection with the laws of the Spirit was made. Vol 1 contains the current draft of the Laws of the Spirit. Most are solid in my opinion, but I am clear about what needs further work. Also, the Periodic Table of Elements in Vol 1, which arranges the laws to look for gaps, is notional and needs testing. One easy test is to see whether the table in fact predicts a new law I had not seen before, but that is there in scripture.

Volume 6 is what makes future development possible beyond my own participation. The three confidence tiers it introduces — anchor claims at 85% and above, working claims at 65–80%, and frontier claims below 65% — each receive different treatment from the Council. The frontier claims (V3.Exp3 Spiritual Force at 40%, V3.Exp6 Conservation Laws at 35%, V3.Exp8 Miracles as threshold events at 55%) move only when research evidence accumulates, not when a well-argued proposal arrives. The annual calibration cycle that flows Volume 4 testing results back into Volumes 1–3 is the mechanism by which the confidence ratings I have assigned today get challenged, confirmed, or revised by the data. That is the posture the whole work is meant to hold: open to revision, structurally disciplined about how revision happens, and accountable to a community of practitioners rather than to any single voice, including mine.

Speaking of voices, you will see multiple voices in these documents. They are originally written in what might be called a kitchen-table voice, me sitting across a kitchen table, talking with my kids and grandkids about some things I have discovered and how things are going. The Governance volume (Vol 6) is in the third-person voice, reflecting a shift from me to an open-source phase and speaking to a community of interested practitioners who have joined the journey and are willing to document their discoveries and respond to what these volumes have started. One key movement across these volumes will be to start hearing a “we” voice as more people share, and what was my speaking starts to become a collective voice of discovery. This is as it should be and, in fact, reflects the very progressive growth proposed and described, both individually and collectively, for this journey. Please know that even when the voice starts to turn to a "us” voice, I am still in it, caring about you, praying for your journey of discovery. I hope you will always hear my heart towards you.

I expect to continue developing this document of discovery as long as I have breath. And when that is done, I’ll get to see how much is true.  I’m sure that much of what I am offering here is gold, silver, and precious stones, but some is also wood, hay, and stubble. I just don’t know for sure which is which at this point, but I will know when I stand face to face with my Lord.

In all this, I am inviting you into an intentional journey of the heart. A journey needs a goal, and in this case, that is into the heart of God, who invites us to work with him in deeper and deeper fellowship and oneness. Scripture describes such a life, and we can respond. I’m on such a journey, and this document captures at least some of that journey. However, you will have your own journey, and that journey will be as individual as your fingerprints.

Please join me in this intentional journey. As C.S. Lewis said in “The Last Battle”, “Further up and further in.” I’ll meet you there.
