# Introduction: What I Am Trying to Do Here

**My Starting Question**

I want to begin with a question that has driven me for over two decades: what if the way the world of the Spirit works is just as logical and discoverable as the laws of physics? Not identical to physics — but equally orderly, equally real, and equally open to investigation by someone willing to pay attention.

Jesus rebuked Nicodemus in John 3, and His rebuke was not about a lack of piety or devotion. Nicodemus was a ruler of the Jews, a serious student of scripture. The rebuke was that he should have been able to understand how things worked in the Spirit, and he couldn’t. That, without doubt, stung a little. If the teacher of Israel should have known, then surely we who have the full canon, the indwelling Holy Spirit, and two thousand years of the church’s experience, should be able to see deeper than we do.

A word of caution to myself before I proceed: the goal of mapping these laws is not to replace the direct experience of them. C.S. Lewis warned that the person who has only a map of the Atlantic has something useful — but the person who has sailed it has something the map-reader cannot get from the paper. What follows is my best map of the territory I have been sailing for over four decades. The map is meant to help you find your way in, not to substitute for the crossing.

That is the starting point. Not arrogance, actually the opposite. It is humility in the face of how much we have been given and how little we have understood.

My initial starting point is the observation that we live a life so far short of what Jesus invites us into. A life of walking in the Spirit, of miracles in the natural, and living in intimate conversation with him.
