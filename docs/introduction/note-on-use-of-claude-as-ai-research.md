# A Note on the use of Claude as an AI research assistant

I have used Claude (and previously ChatGPT) as a research assistant to take my hundreds of pages of research, thinking, and, in some cases, just journal notes, with a wide range of insights and speculations, to organize this all into a coherent, hopefully simplified discussion. Some of the text is directly from me, and some was revised by Claude. I have used multiple AI tools to cross-check and peer review/challenge others’ outputs with good results. For what it's worth, I have found Grammarly to be a lifesaver in simplifying, clarifying, and correcting my grammar, syntax, punctuation, and spelling. I naturally write in convoluted, indirect sentences that generally wander around a bunch of topics. This mostly reflects my internal thinking process and is, without doubt, painful for any readers. AI tools have certainly helped in that regard.

In every case, I edited everything just as I would with any other source. In the case of an AI assistant, my experience is that the outputs sometimes remind me of a friend who has had too much wine and starts spouting off while we are brainstorming ideas. I take ownership of the current result as reflecting my current understanding and certainty of each law and proposition.
