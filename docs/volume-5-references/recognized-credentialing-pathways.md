# Recognized Credentialing Pathways

The Formation Companion role benefits from but is not defined by formal credentialing. A Companion I operating in group mode within a FotH may be trained entirely through the FotH's own internal apprenticeship, without external credentialing, and still serve well. A Companion II or III typically has at least one external credential in their primary mode, because the training depth required to practice that mode at working depth exceeds what internal training can usually provide.

The recognized credentialing pathways, by mode:

### Spiritual Direction
- Two- and three-year programs accredited by Spiritual Directors International (SDI) or equivalent institutional recognition. Examples: Shalem Institute, Spiritual Formation Academy, Oasis Ministries, Christos Center, denominational programs (Jesuit, Benedictine, Catholic seminary-based; various Protestant equivalents).
### Transformation Prayer Ministry and Related Memory-Healing Modalities
- TPM's own facilitator certification through the Aslan Group; Theophostic training (historical predecessor); Sozo training (Bethel); Restoring the Foundations facilitator certification through the Kylstra ministries.
### Stephen-Style Lay Care
- Stephen Ministry's formal Leader's Training Course (one week residential) and ongoing local training for Stephen Ministers (fifty hours).
### Group Mode Deep-Process Work
- Shadow Work facilitator certification through Shadow Work Seminars (recognized as a non-Christian-grounded methodology to be used within a Christian framework per the discerning-use section above); inner healing training through schools such as Pastoral Care Ministries (Leanne Payne), Elijah House, or equivalent; group facilitation training through secular programs (e.g., Institute of Cultural Affairs) for the container-forming and group-dynamics competencies.
No single pathway is required. What is required is that a Companion II or III has received training at working depth in at least one of these pathways for their primary mode and is in ongoing supervision with a practitioner more mature than themselves.
