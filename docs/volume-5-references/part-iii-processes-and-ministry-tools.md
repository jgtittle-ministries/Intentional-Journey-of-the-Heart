# Part III: Processes and Ministry Tools

## Grief Recovery Handbook (GRH)
**Author: **John W. James and Russell Friedman are co-founders of the Grief Recovery Institute and co-authors of the *Grief Recovery Handbook*, first published in 1988. The GRH is the foundational text of the Grief Recovery Method, a structured process for completing unresolved grief through a sequence of exercises culminating in a completion letter that addresses the specific unfinished emotional communications attached to a loss or wound.

**Summary: **The GRH process identifies grief as any unresolved, incomplete emotional response to a loss — including losses of relationship, trust, safety, innocence, health, and life stage, not only bereavement. Its central insight is that grief knots form when the emotional response to a loss is not completed: the person moves forward in life but the emotional energy of the loss remains active, consuming bandwidth and shaping behavior below the level of conscious awareness. The completion letter process creates a structured opportunity to name and release those unfinished communications.

**Application: **Vol 2, Exp. 2 (Emotional Knots — The Energetic Blockage Law) identifies grief knots as one of the four primary types of emotional blockage and specifies the GRH completion letter process as the primary tool for their resolution. The Tool Map (Vol 2, Exp. 6) pairs GRH explicitly with grief knots and notes that the spiritual completion of GRH work often happens through a subsequent TPM session: GRH names and completes the emotional content, and TPM provides the access point for the Holy Spirit to complete the healing. Vol 4 references the GRH process as a Seasonal practice in the Training Plan.

## Transformation Prayer Ministry (TPM)
**Author: **Ed Smith is the founder and developer of Transformation Prayer Ministry (TPM), a structured prayer ministry approach built on the premise that emotional pain in the present is driven by core beliefs formed in the past. Smith developed TPM over decades of pastoral and counseling practice, and the method has been trained to thousands of practitioners internationally. TPM is designed to give the Holy Spirit access to specific memories and lies that are producing current emotional distress.

**Summary: **TPM works from the conviction that emotional pain is the trail leading to a core belief (a lie believed about oneself, God, or others) formed in the context of a specific past experience. The TPM process guides the person to follow the emotion back to its source memory, identify the lie embedded in that memory, and then invite the Holy Spirit to speak His truth to that specific place. The process is explicitly non-directive with respect to what the Spirit will say — the facilitator’s role is to create access, not to provide the answer.

**Application: **TPM is one of the central tools in the LotS Vol 2 framework for clearing cognitive blockages (Exp. 3: Believing a Lie — The Cognitive Blockage Law). Vol 2 describes TPM as creating the access point for the Spirit to address the lie at the root of fear, shame, and anger knots. The Tool Map (Exp. 6) assigns TPM to multiple knot types and notes its relationship to GRH: the two processes address complementary dimensions (emotional completion and cognitive renewal respectively). Vol 2 notes that the lie-at-the-root mechanism was confirmed through the author’s own TPM experience, providing personal evidence for LotS claim LotS-C1.
