# Hearing and Obeying — The Developmental Spine

One move from the original Weapons Master paper requires explicit correction in this paper: the original treated scriptural engagement and hearing-and-obeying God as prerequisites (checkbox items the Companion either has or does not have). Scriptural engagement and hearing-and-obeying, however, are not prerequisites; they are the developmental spine of the Companion role, and they continue to develop across all three levels of Companion maturity.

Operationally, this means the Companion's own Affective Taxonomy stage, as defined in HFT, is not a one-time checkpoint but a continuously developing variable that governs what the Companion can and cannot do. A Companion at Stage 3 can Companion I. A Companion at Stage 4 can become a Companion II. A Companion moving toward Stage 5 is approaching Companion III maturity. The stage is not an honorific; it is an operational measure of the Companion's capacity to model what they lead.

HFT also describes the PROAPT method as the primary practice for moving the Companion through their own stage progression. A Companion who is not themselves engaged in PROAPT (or an equivalent disciplined scriptural practice) has stopped developing, and their capacity to companion others will begin to erode even if their skills-level competencies remain. The Companion's own PROAPT practice is not a tool they teach; it is the ground they walk. The Council's ongoing testing work (Vol 4) treats the Companion's own Affective Taxonomy stage as an explicit independent variable for exactly this reason.

Practical corollary: every Companion has their own PROAPT practice, their own spiritual direction or supervision, and their own peer accountability group. These three together form the minimum scaffolding that keeps the Companion developing. A Companion missing any one of the three is vulnerable to drift — not because their training has failed, but because formation development does not sustain without ongoing practice.
