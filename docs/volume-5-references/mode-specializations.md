Each of the four modes adds specific competencies on top of the common core. What follows is a brief description of each mode's specialized content. This paper does not attempt to be a training curriculum for any of the four, as each has its own literature and training infrastructure, and no single paper could replace them. The intent here is to show what each mode contributes to the Formation Companion role as a specialization.

## Spiritual Direction Mode
The Spiritual Direction mode specialization is defined primarily by its posture of attending and its rhythm of long-term, infrequent, unhurried meetings (typically monthly, sometimes quarterly). The added competencies are contemplative listening (listening for what God is doing under what the directee is saying), the use of silence as an active practice rather than a pause, the specific questions of the Ignatian tradition (what consolation, what desolation, what is God inviting), and a light touch that invites without prescribing. The training pathway is typically a two- or three-year program through a Spiritual Directors International-recognized institution. The Companion operating in SD mode typically meets with a small number of directees over a long-time horizon, often years.

## TPM-Style Mode
The TPM-style specialization is the most protocol-specific and the most narrowly targeted. The added competencies are the identification of emotional pain tied to specific memories, the discernment of the lie embedded in each memory, the framing of prayer for Jesus to reveal truth into the memory, and the handling of resistance and multi-session work on recurring patterns. The training pathway is TPM's own facilitator certification, or equivalent training in related memory-healing modalities (Theophostic, Restoring the Foundations, Sozo, Leanne Payne). The Companion operating in TPM-style mode typically works one-on-one on specific targeted sessions rather than ongoing long-term companioning.

## Stephen-Style Mode
The Stephen-style specialization is the mode of walking alongside a pilgrim through an extended season of crisis, loss, transition, or hardship. The added competencies are the discipline of Christian presence without an agenda, the handling of grief and acute distress, the management of long-term low-intensity relationships that can span years, and the institutional practices around assignment, confidentiality, and supervision that Stephen Ministry has developed. Training is through Stephen Ministry's own fifty-hour curriculum or equivalent. The Companion operating in Stephen-style mode typically works one-on-one with a care receiver through a defined season, not indefinitely.

## Group Mode (Band of Brothers / Fellowship of the Heart)
The group mode specialization is the most tool-diverse and, in part, the most dynamically complex, due in part to multiple participants. The added competencies are as follows:

- container theory and processes, i.e., how to form a group in which the Spirit's work can unfold safely;
- the full Level I tool set listed in the tool inventory below;
- the Level II deep-process tools (Guts facilitation, Voice Dialogue, Inner Healing, Paradox Holding, and others), reading group dynamics in real time; and
- the capacity to hold the group's formation at the same time as each individual's.
Training is through apprenticeship in an existing group, plus formal training in specific deep-process methodologies. The Companion operating in group mode typically leads a group of six to twelve pilgrims over months to years, with the group itself being the primary unit of formation.
