# Conclusion

The Formation Companion is a synthesis role, now defined as a category in this paper, though the reality of its names has existed as long as believers have walked with one another. Four established Christian care traditions, Spiritual Direction, Transformation Prayer Ministry, Stephen Ministry, and group facilitation in the Band of Brothers and Fellowship of the Heart contexts have developed mostly independently of one another for decades or centuries. When laid side by side, they converge on a substantial common core and theme. That core centers on the essence of the Formation Companion role. The mode specializations are what make the role operational in a given moment. The capstone competency is the real-time discernment of which mode serves the moment.

This paper provides a starting framework, i.e., a vocabulary, structure, and a first pass at an architecture that can withstand testing, argument, and refinement, and settle into a structure that the Fellowship of the Heart can actually use. The Formation Companion role has always existed, and this paper is a first attempt to name what has always been there so that training for it can be done intentionally rather than disparately.

The author holds this paper at 50% confidence as a first draft with forthcoming substantial revisions. There are likely tools, nuances, and modes missed. The synthesis framing is new and has not yet been tested. Further, the three-taxonomy 3 × 3 matrix is a structural proposal that needs to be workshopped with practitioners before becoming a training curriculum. The tool inventory adaptations need the tacit judgment that only years of practice can supply. The character prerequisites section requires additional work beyond this paper, which likely means the next version will be significantly different. In sum, the wider Laws of the Spirit that motivates this paper has always been an open exploration. This paper is explicitly an invitation to the next round of that exploration.
