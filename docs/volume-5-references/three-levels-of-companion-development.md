# The Three Levels of Companion Development

Companion development is proposed as a three-level progression. The levels are cumulative: Companion II includes Companion I, and Companion III includes both. Each level represents substantial growth in all three taxonomy domains, not just one. The levels are deliberately named so that no level sounds like or remains as a permanent station. A Companion at Level I is not lesser; he or she is at the beginning of a long journey that a lifetime may or may not complete.

## Companion I — Protocol Competent
The Level I Companion can engage simple, repeatable tools and practices with fidelity. The tools at this level have been selected because they are sufficiently robust to produce real transformation and simple enough to be reproduced by anyone who has seen them well-led and received competent training. This is the Navigators' memory-incentivized reproducibility principle applied to formation tools. A new Fellowship of the Heart can be sustained for a long time by a group of Level I Companions alone, if they use the tools well and continue to grow. Some Companions will remain at Level I for their entire life of service, and that is honorable.

The Affective Taxonomy floor for Level I is Stage 3 — the scripture-is-personally-binding threshold from HFT. A Companion below Stage 3 is not ready to companion at Level I, even if they have learned the protocols. They can still be in the room, still be growing, still be part of a FotH group, but they should not be the ones leading the protocol.

## Companion II — Mode Practitioner
The Level II Companion has developed at least one mode specialization at working depth, i.e., Spiritual Direction, TPM-style work, Stephen-style care, or deep group process facilitation beyond the Level I protocols. This requires formal training in that mode (typically through a credentialing pathway, see below), sustained supervised practice, and integration of the mode's specific tools into the Companion's own discernment. A Level II Companion is also able to lead from behind, to help a pilgrim own their own next steps rather than prescribing the path.

The Affective Taxonomy floor for Level II is Stage 4, the values-integrated threshold, where the Companion's own life has been substantially reorganized around scripture and the Companion can model what that reorganization looks like from the inside, not just describe it from the outside. Level II work pulls on whatever unfinished business the Companion holds, and a Stage 3 Companion will not have the integration to hold the weight.

## Companion III — Integrated Practitioner
The Level III Companion has two or more mode specializations at working depth and has developed the capstone competency center for the interest of this paper: the real-time discernment of which mode a given moment requires, and the capacity to move between modes without losing grounding. The Level III Companion can also train other Companions. This can include certifying Level I, mentoring Level II toward Level III, and helping the Council (or other oversight body) assess whether a particular Companion is ready for a given level.

The Affective Taxonomy floor for Level III is Stage 4 or higher, and on the journey to Stage 5 over time. Stage 5 is the characterization stage, where the Companion's whole life has become coherent around their formation, and the modeling effect on others has become largely unconscious because it is simply who the Companion is. Few Companions reach Stage 5. For some, that is as it should be.

## The 3 × 3 Development Matrix
The three taxonomies crossed with the three levels produce a 3 × 3 matrix that makes the developmental progression specific. Each cell names what a Companion at that level must have developed along that taxonomy dimension. The matrix is read horizontally (what develops across levels within one domain) and vertically (what develops at each level across all three domains).
