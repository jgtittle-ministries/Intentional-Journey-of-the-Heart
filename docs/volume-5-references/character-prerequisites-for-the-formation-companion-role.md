# Character Prerequisites for the Formation Companion Role

The character prerequisites for a Christian formation role in a mixed-gender context, across four tradition streams, i.e., Reformed, Evangelical, Charismatic, Mystical, and with appropriate scriptural grounding, is territory that requires focused theological work. This section will be revised in consultation with the Council.

What the section will address in a later version of this paper:

- The character qualifications of 1 Timothy 3:1-13 and Titus 1:5-9 (traditionally applied to elders and deacons), read in the broader NT context.
- The Ephesians 4:11-12 gifting for the equipping of the saints, which names the role of the equipper explicitly, without gender restriction.
- The spiritual gifting passages of Romans 12:6-8 and 1 Corinthians 12, which enumerate gifts without gendering them.
- Explicit NT examples of women in formation and teaching roles: Priscilla instructing Apollos in Acts 18:26; Phoebe as *diakonos* in Romans 16:1; the Titus 2:3-5 pattern of older women teaching younger women; Lydia as the first European convert and likely house-church host in Acts 16.
- The practical pattern of the four traditional streams the IJH project draws on, each of which has its own approach to women's leadership in formation roles.
- The specific character markers that apply in any tradition, such as integrity of life, stability of marriage (where married) or singleness held well, absence of addictive patterns, gentleness, non-violence, hospitality, apt to teach, not a novice, good reputation with outsiders, and the other character markers of the pastoral epistles applied in the gender-inclusive register the NT itself demonstrates when read carefully.
