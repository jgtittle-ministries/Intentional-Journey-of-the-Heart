# Tool Inventory — First Pass

The tool inventory below is a first pass adaptation of the Weapons Master paper's tool list for the mixed-gender Fellowship of the Heart context. It is presented as a draft for review and workshop testing rather than a finished curriculum. Some tools are kept as-is because their names and content are already gender-neutral; some have naming notes where the current name carries masculine framing even though the underlying work is universal. Some have framing notes where the tool is usable across genders but should be led with explicit attention to how it will land differently. This section assumes ongoing workshopping with practitioners rather than settlement in a single pass.

## Level I Tools
These are the protocols a Companion I can lead with fidelity after having seen them well-led and received the training. They are simple enough to be reproducible and rich enough to produce real transformation. This is the Navigators' reproducibility principle in practice. Detailed protocols will be provided later.

- **Social Contract** — Keep as-is. Gender-neutral in name and content.
- **Container Forming** — Keep as-is. Gender-neutral.
- **Clearings** — Keep as-is. Gender-neutral.
- **Tell Your Story** — Keep as-is. Gender-neutral. Note: the specific invitations within the process may need to be framed to honor that stories from women's and men's lives often turn on different formative experiences; the tool itself works the same way.
- **Who Are You** — Keep as-is. The question of identity is universal.
- **Heart Bible Study** — Keep as-is. The PROAPT method described in HFT is gender-neutral in design.
- **Shadow Mission** — Keep, with note. The term "shadow" is borrowed from Jungian psychology; the tool itself helps a pilgrim identify the inverse of their true mission — the life they are tempted to live instead of the one God is calling them to. Framing note: Shadow missions are as real for women as for men; the content of shadow missions tends to differ in gendered ways, which the Companion should be aware of without scripting.
- **Mission Clarity and Stretch** — Keep as-is. Universal; applied identically.
- **Hot Seat** — Keep the name. The term has a masculine-coded history in the men's work movement, but it is descriptive (the pilgrim is in the hot seat of the group's focused attention) and is widely used in group facilitation contexts for mixed genders. Note: the Companion leading must explicitly hold safety for the person in the seat in a gender-aware way.
- **What's at Risk** — Keep as-is.
- **Want-Resist** — Keep as-is. The internal tension the tool works with is universal.
- **Group Mission** — Keep as-is.
- **Heart Prayer Process** — Keep as-is.
- **Council of Elders** — Review. The phrase council of elders carries a masculine connotation in some Christian traditions; in others, it is gender-neutral. Options: keep as-is with framing, rename to Wisdom Council, or provide both names and let the specific FotH choose.
- **7 × 7** — Keep as-is (assuming it is the specific process named in the Weapons Master paper).
- **Kingdom of God** — Keep as-is.
- **Mini Hot Seat** — Same notes as Hot Seat.
## Level II Processes
These are the deep-process tools that require formal training, skills practice, and supervised mentorship before a Companion II can lead them. They are not list items for Companion I to attempt after reading a description.

- **Guts Facilitation** — Keep the name with framing. Guts, as a term, has masculine-coded history in the men's work movement, but is descriptive of somatic emotional processing and has gender-neutral versions in practice. The Companion leading Guts work with mixed-gender groups should have received explicit training in how the work lands differently for women and men and how to hold the container accordingly.
- **Shadow Work Version** — Keep as-is. Bilgere's work has mixed-gender applications that are well developed.
- **Dmitri's Version / Guts Commando** — Rename needed. Commando is masculine-coded and martial in a way that does not carry into FotH. Propose: Dmitri's Intensive Guts Process, or Dmitri's Variant, or similar.
- **Voice Dialogue** — Keep as-is. Stone and Stone's Psychology of Selves is gender-neutral in design.
- **Paradox Holding** — Keep as-is.
- **Big Mind Process** — Keep as-is, with note that the Big Mind framework has non-Christian metaphysical roots (Zen Buddhist) and should be used with the same discerning framework that applies to Shadow Work above.
- **Inner Healing** — Keep as-is. The inner healing tradition (Leanne Payne, Rita Bennett — already in Vol 5) is mixed-gender in origin and application.
- **Restoring the Foundations (RTF)** — Keep as-is. Chester and Betsy Kylstra's ministry is mixed-gender in design.
- **Emotionally Free** — Keep as-is.
