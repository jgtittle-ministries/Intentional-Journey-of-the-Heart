# Governance

The governance framework for *Intentional Journey of the Heart* is the
project's **Volume 6: Governance**, authored by John G. Tittle.

The canonical text lives in this repository at:

> [`source-documents/v5_5_7/IJH_Vol6_v5_5_7_1.docx`](source-documents/v5_5_7/IJH_Vol6_v5_5_7_1.docx)

Volume 6 establishes:

- A three-tier authority structure (Founder/Editor-in-Chief, Council of
  Stewards, Contributor body)
- A claim-registry-driven editorial process built around the YAML registry
  files in `source-documents/v5_5_7/` (`vol1-claims.yml` through
  `vol4-claims.yml`)
- Three confidence tiers (anchor / working / frontier) with distinct
  procedures for each
- Decision-making framework (lazy consensus → consent-based approval →
  two-thirds supermajority for governance amendments)
- Bidirectional dependency tracking with annual calibration cycles
- Succession planning with staged founder-to-Council transition

## Companion documents

Volume 6 references three companion documents drafted alongside it that
together form the procedural substrate of the governance framework:

- `CONTRIBUTING.md` — contributor onboarding, voice, and mechanics
- `PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md` — structured template for proposed claim refinements
- `SUCCESSION_LETTER.md` — Literary Executor and Theological Successor designations

These will be committed to the repository when ratified. A `SCHEMA.md`
defining the claim-registry schema will be drafted from the implicit
specification in the registry files as the Council's first substantive
ratification vote.

## Status

This governance framework is currently in pre-Council operation: the
founder is exercising stewardship directly, with the registry serving as
the authoritative working record. The Council of Stewards is to be seated
on the timeline described in Volume 6 §12 and §13.
